HISTORY

Manaranjan fort is one of the two hill forts that constitute the Rajmachi fortification, the other being Shrivardhan Fort.[1] It is located in Rajmachi village which 8.5 km from Lonavala in the Sahyadris mountain range. Manaranjan fort is 2700 feet high which makes it lower than the neighbouring Shreevardhan fort. It has three gates and large ramparts. From here Karnala fort can be seen.[2] There are some water tanks at the summit. Early History - Yadava Dynasty: The fort is believed to have been constructed during the rule of the Yadava dynasty, which reigned over the region from the 9th to 14th centuries Strategic Location: Manaranjan Fort's strategic location overlooking the Bor Ghat, a crucial trade route connecting the Konkan coast with the Deccan plateau, made it an important military outpost. 2.Maratha Period-Shivaji Maharaj:The fort came under the control of the Maratha king Shivaji Maharaj in the mid-17th century. It was one of the many forts he captured during his efforts to establish the Maratha Empire. -Defense System: Shivaji Maharaj strengthened the fort's defenses, recognizing its strategic importance in protecting the Maratha territories from invading forces. 3.Peshwa Era Administratio: During the Peshwa period, the fort continued to serve as a military stronghold and administrative center. The Peshwas were prime ministers in the Maratha Empire, and they maintained and used several forts, including Manaranjan, for governance and defense. 4British Colonial Period Capture by the British: The fort, along with others in the region, eventually fell to the British East India Company in the early 19th century during their campaign to conquer the Maratha territories. -Decline: Post its capture, the fort saw a decline in its strategic importance. The British focused on other, more accessible forts and military outposts.Architectural Features -Ramparts and Bastions: Manaranjan Fort boasts robust ramparts and several bastions, reflecting typical Maratha military architecture designed to withstand sieges and attacks. -Water Tanks: There are several water tanks within the fort precincts, which were essential for sustaining the garrison stationed there. Gates and Watchtowers: The fort's gates and watchtowers provided defense and surveillance capabilities, allowing the occupants to monitor the surrounding areas for enemy movements
